# Blockchain Basics – Web3 Learning Path

A beginner-friendly blockchain learning roadmap covering fundamentals, architecture, and real-world use cases, with a focus on India.

---

## Who is this for?

This learning path is designed for:
- Students new to blockchain and Web3
- Engineering / CS students
- Developers transitioning into Web3
- Tech-curious learners exploring blockchain use cases in India

No prior blockchain experience required.

---

## What you will learn

By the end of this path, you will understand:
- How blockchain works under the hood
- Core concepts like decentralization, consensus, and cryptography
- Differences between public, private, and consortium blockchains
- How blockchain is applied in finance, governance, and India’s digital ecosystem

---

## Curriculum Overview

---

## Tutorial 1: Blockchain Fundamentals and Architecture

- What is a Blockchain? (basic definition and analogy)
- History of Blockchain and Bitcoin (origin story)
- Core components: blocks, chains, and transactions
- Decentralization and peer-to-peer networks
- Consensus mechanisms (Proof of Work, Proof of Stake, etc.)
- Cryptography in blockchain (hashing, digital signatures)
- Public vs. Private vs. Consortium blockchains
- Blockchain data structure (Merkle trees, block headers)
- Roles in blockchain (miners/validators, full nodes)
- Blockchain vs. Traditional Databases (key differences)
- Immutability and security properties
- Smart contracts basics (introduction to self-executing code)
- Benefits of blockchain (security, transparency, trustlessness)
- Challenges and limitations (scalability, energy use, regulation)
- Key blockchain use-cases (finance, supply chain, identity, etc.)
- Popular platforms (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Hyperledger, etc.)
- Network nodes (full nodes vs. light wallets)
- Basics of blockchain wallets and addresses

---

## Tutorial 2: How Blockchain Works (Technical Deep Dive)

- Anatomy of a blockchain transaction (inputs, outputs)
- Transaction validation and propagation in a P2P network
- Mining and block creation process (Proof of Work)
- Block reward and incentives for miners
- Alternative consensus (Proof of Stake, Delegated PoS, Byzantine Fault Tolerance)
- Difficulty adjustment and block time (Bitcoin example)
- Transaction confirmations and finality (preventing double-spend)
- Smart contract execution within blocks (Ethereum example)
- Gas and transaction fees (Ethereum gas model)
- Blocks and forks (soft forks vs. hard forks)
- Consensus protocol upgrades (e.g. Ethereum’s Merge)
- Using block explorers (tracking transactions and blocks)
- Example walkthrough: Sending a transaction on Bitcoin or Ethereum
- Node synchronization (initial sync, bloom filters)

---

## Tutorial 3: Blockchain Applications and Use Cases in India

- Government blockchain initiatives (DigiLocker, land registry, supply chain)
- National Strategy on Blockchain (NITI Aayog’s vision)
- Blockchain in banking and finance (payments, remittances)
- Agriculture and food supply chain (traceability, farm-to-fork)
- Healthcare and pharmaceuticals (drug tracking, DAVA project)
- Identity and public records (Aadhaar integration, certificates)
- India’s Digital Rupee (Central Bank Digital Currency plans)
- State-level projects (Telangana, Andhra Pradesh blockchain policies)
- Academia and research (IITs, NITI Aayog research initiatives)
- Industry use-cases (TCS/Infosys blockchain solutions)
- Fintech adoption (digital KYC, insurance claims)
- Startups ecosystem (e.g. Polygon, WazirX, CoinDCX)
- Collaboration and hackathons (India Blockchain Summit)
- Future trends and opportunities in India’s blockchain adoption
- Challenges in India (infrastructure, awareness, regulatory hurdles)
